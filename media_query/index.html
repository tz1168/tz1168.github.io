Where to go

Sanlintun is an area of the Chaoyang District, Beijing containing many popular bar streets and international stores. It is a popular destination for shopping, dining, and entertainment. This is where Chinese young adults spend their night out and hangout with friends. The modern buildings and unique cafes and restaurants are always fresh to discover. You can basically find everything you need here: there are global brands like adidas and uniqlo, but also chinese brands you might not know yet; there are famous restaurants that is globally known, but also unique restaurants that's small but worth tasting; there are big movies theatres, bars and crowded night clubs, but also quiet bookstores, cafes and bakerys.

Nanluoguxiang is a narrow alley, that gives its name to an old part of the Beijing city centre, that has traditional architecture both new and old. The neighborhood contains many typical narrow streets known as hutong. It is located in the Dongcheng district. The alley itself is approximately 800m long, running from East Gulou Street in the north to Di'anmen East Street in the south. It is not just a narrow street, along the way you will see all kinds of different street food and small shops and cafes. One of the most worth trying dessert in my opinion, is a traditional Chinese bakery called WenYuShuangPiNai. The milk custard with red beans is my must have whenever I go there.

The Place (Shimaotianjie) is located at No. 9 Guanghua Road, Chaoyang District, Beijing; east side of No.12 Dongdaqiao Road. It is a new behemoth, marked by a huge outdoor screen playing clips of random fashion shows, sees plenty of fashionista traffic. Spanish retailer Zara chose to set up shop here; their arrival in Beijing was highly anticipated by locals and expats alike. Make-up gurus MAC also chose The place for their flagship store. Other retailers like French Connection, Mango, and Adidas ensure this place is virtually bargain hunter-free. Other than shopping, many people go to The Place to hang out because during the night there are beautiful lights and differnt installations during different time period.

Secret tips

1. Don't go to the places where only foreigners are present.

2. Check several stores for price or look at the tags yourself, there are bad merchants!!!(They will ask foreigners for higher price because they don't have a sense of what is reasonable)

3. Google Map is a must have.

4. The Subway is usually faster than the roads because Beijing has heavy traffic(even till midnight).

5. Ask if you are concerned about something. Usually young adults speak English.
