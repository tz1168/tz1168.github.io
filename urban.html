Project Intro

Have you ever experienced any of the following scenarios? Have you ever been in a group chat with 999+ messages unread? And as you try to catch up and go through the chat, you see phrases and slangs you don’t understand? Or maybe you are at a lit party having conversations with a bunch of people. Everyone seems to be having fun but you don't understand a joke everyone's laughing at?

Like when you know each words meaning, but when they get together, it just makes no sense. You might try to figure it now by yourself but it’s usually unsolvable. However, it seems like everyone else all know the meaning and you are already too afraid to ask.

You might have heard of urban dictionary before. In fact, it might already be one of the most commonly used sources on the internet. But there’s still a lot that can be changed and updated, especially their mobile app.

The Problem

Problem Statement

People like international students studying in the U.S have trouble with understanding American Culture (slang, jokes, references, etc...), which can be resolved by a dictionary app to learn the meaning and usage.

We know we’re right when more international students download our app.

Feature Prioritization

  • Must
    Be able to help users find the exact meaning of slangs, jokes, references, etc.
    Be easy to navigate
  • Should
    Be able to identify certified definitions
    Be able to provide background information/examples of the terms
    Be able to bookmark the information as future reference
    Be accessible
  • Could
    Have a real time trending terms list
    Have translation to different languages
    Create a community
  • Won't
    Overdesign
    Have lots of advertisements
The Research

Quantitative Survey

Results from the survey show that more people tend not to ask questions in the moment. The reason behind it is mainly not wanting to interrupt the conversation, followed by feeling too awkward to ask, and about 40% of the people I interviewed said they did not want others to know that they do not know the thing. So a solution is needed, and the methods people are currently using is obviously google, other keywords like online, internet, and search were also brought up frequently. A few respondent mentioned that they feel more comfortable asking friends separately.

I want to point out here that 100% of the respondents said they have used dictionary apps to search for meanings of slangs/memes/quotes/acronyms.
